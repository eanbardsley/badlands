The book Seven Arrows deals with Medicine Wheels. When I worked in astronomy at the state observatory at Pine Mountain in the high desert East of Bend, Oregon, up on the hill behind the telescopes was a Medicine Wheel that one of the graduate students in Astronomy built. A medicine wheel is a Native American version of England’s Stonehenge in Wiltshire the epicenter of crop circles. Albeit the stones are much smaller (they still can’t explain how the druids who built Stonehenge hoisted the enormous rocks and stacked them) but the Medicine Wheel has many of the same alignments, like stones pointing from its center at the position where the sun sets at summer solstice, fall equinox, winter solstice and when the sun swings down back to the fall equinox position it is spring equinox.
